## Digital Tools & Platforms in Norwegian Aquaculture

A practical inventory of the actual software/platforms in use; who built them, what they do, and how they're deployed. This complements the data-domain documents (which describe *what* data exists) by mapping *what systems generate, move, and manage* that data.

**Deployment column key:** Cloud/web-based = accessed via browser, vendor-hosted · On-prem/local = runs on farm-local hardware · Hybrid = both.

---

## Commercial Vendor Platforms

| Tool / Platform | Primary Domain(s) | Services / Data Activities | Built & Owned By | Deployment | IoT/Sensor Integration | Scale of Use / Status |
|-----------------|-------------------|----------------------------|------------------|------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| **AKVA Fishtalk** (now "AKVA Fishtalk 5") | Production Planning, Fish Health, Feed | Full production/biological and financial planning, egg-to-harvest traceability, batch tracking, reporting; recently rebuilt as cloud-based with open APIs | AKVA group (Norway) | Cloud-based (migrated from on-prem in recent relaunch), browser + mobile app | Yes; connects to AKVA's own sensor, biomass, camera, and lice-counting systems (Connect 4, Submerged, Observe) via open APIs | Reported ~60% market share among Norwegian/international salmon farmers; one of the longest-established systems in the industry |
| **AKVA Connect** | Production Planning, Feed | Broader digital ecosystem/dashboard layer integrating feeding, sensors, cameras across AKVA's hardware and software lines | AKVA group (Norway) | Cloud-based | Yes; designed as the integration layer across AKVA's own hardware | Deployed across AKVA's global customer base; positioned as AKVA's flagship "digital" segment product |
| **Observe** (AKVA Observe) | Feed, Fish Health | AI feeding-behavior analysis using existing pen cameras — detects appetite, pellet waste, feeding efficiency in real time | Originally Observe Technologies (Norway/UK); fully acquired by AKVA group in 2024 | Cloud-based, integrates with existing camera hardware (no new equipment required) | Yes; designed specifically to plug into a farm's existing camera/IoT infrastructure | Marketed as reducing FCR and improving feeding response; integrated directly into Fishtalk 5 |
| **ScaleAQ Mercatus suite** (Mercatus Farmer, Finance, Future, Vet) | Production Planning, Feed, Fish Health | Farmer: biomass/production documentation & traceability. Finance: cost/inventory calculation. Future: production & harvest scenario planning. Vet: fish health records, prescriptions, veterinary reporting | ScaleAQ (Norway) | Cloud-based, open APIs, browser + mobile | Yes; integrates with ScaleAQ's FeedStation, Vision, Barge Control feeding hardware | Used by major Norwegian farmers including Mowi (renewed partnership Oct 2025); central to Mowi's "MOWInsight" internal analytics platform |
| **ScaleAQ FeedStation / Vision / Barge Control / Knowledger** | Feed | Precision feeding control, surface/feeding camera monitoring, feed barge remote control, appetite-condition tracking | ScaleAQ (Norway) | Hybrid; on-site hardware with cloud reporting | Yes — these ARE the sensor/IoT layer feeding into Mercatus | Widely deployed across ScaleAQ's Norwegian customer base |
| **Aquabyte (HYDRA 360)** | Fish Health, Feed | Computer-vision camera system: sea lice counting, biomass estimation, appetite/welfare detection, individual fish tracking via spot-pattern recognition | Aquabyte Inc., founded 2017 by Bryton Shang (San Francisco, with a major Bergen, Norway office) | Cloud-based (User Portal), camera hardware on-site | Yes; core product IS an IoT camera + cloud ML pipeline | Processed "more than a petabyte" of images and scanned 2.5M+ fish early on; positions itself as market leader; deployed across major Norwegian salmon/trout farms |
| **Tidal** (Orca camera, Tidal Lice Control) | Fish Health, Feed | Underwater AI camera system: biomass, sea lice detection, welfare monitoring, autonomous feeding control; newer "Lice Control" module aims to neutralize lice without manual handling | Tidal (Alphabet/Google X spin-off, independent since July 2024); partnered early with Mowi; recent partnership announced with SalMar | Cloud-based (Google Cloud infrastructure), camera/robotics hardware on-site | Yes-fully integrated camera + sensor + robotics system, built for autonomous operation | Backed by 100+ patents; trained on data from "hundreds of commercial pens"; Lice Control system in field testing with commercial rollout targeted 2026 |
| **BioSort / iFarm** | Fish Health, Production Planning | Individual fish recognition/sorting system — scans fish as they surface for air, enabling per-fish lice count, growth, and mortality tracking without netting | BioSort (technology developer) in partnership with Cermaq (operator) and ScaleAQ (delivery/hardware) | On-prem hardware (submerged sensor/portal system) with software reporting layer | Yes-the system's entire premise is sensor-based individual fish scanning | Trial system at Cermaq sites in Norway; four development licenses awarded (2019); ongoing R&D-stage deployment rather than industry-wide |
| **AquaCloud** | Fish Health (sea lice specifically) | Industry-wide shared database pooling sea lice counts, environmental sensor data, and treatment outcomes across competing companies, using AI (originally IBM Watson) to forecast lice outbreaks ~2 weeks in advance | NCE Seafood Innovation Cluster consortium (Mowi, Lerøy, Grieg Seafood, SalMar, and others); Norwegian government co-funding (Innovation Norway, Siva) | Cloud-based, centralized database | Yes — ingested data from ~2,800 cages/320 sites, drawing on underwater environmental sensors (oxygen, salinity, temperature) | **Discontinued** — the company entered liquidation/administration in January 2026 after ~10 years of operation, despite reporting it had "no absolute limits" ambitions to expand beyond lice into general fish health and environmental data standardization |
| **Manolin** | Fish Health | AI-driven data intelligence platform aggregating farm data for fish health/disease insight | Manolin (originates from Canada, expanded internationally) | Cloud-based | Yes—designed to ingest data from multiple existing farm systems/sensors | Positions itself as a leading aquaculture data intelligence platform; used by fish farms and suppliers internationally, including in Norway |
| **BarentsWatch — Fish Health (Fiskehelse) / AquaInfo** | Fish Health, Production Planning (regulatory) | Public, open-data platform aggregating sea lice prevalence, disease outbreaks (PD, ISA), MAB, site locations/permits, and sustainability indicators nationally | Norwegian government (Ministry of Trade, Industry and Fisheries; built by Kongsberg Spacetec); data sourced from Norwegian Directorate of Fisheries, Mattilsynet, Veterinary Institute, Statistics Norway | Web-based, public API (NLOD open license) | Partial — aggregates *reported* farm data rather than pulling directly from live sensors; update cadence is typically weekly, not real-time | National-scale, all Norwegian aquaculture sites; the definitive public regulatory data source, distinct from any single company's operational system |
| **Genomic selection software (ASReml, BLUPF90 family)** | Selective Breeding & Genetics | Mixed-model statistical software computing BLUP/GBLUP breeding values from pedigree, phenotype, and genomic data | ASReml: VSN International (UK); BLUPF90: University of Georgia (US) — both general-purpose animal-breeding tools, not aquaculture-specific or Norwegian-built | On-prem/local statistical software (not cloud/web platforms) | No direct sensor integration — consumes structured phenotype/genotype data files, doesn't connect to farm IoT itself | Used internally by breeding companies (e.g., AquaGen, Benchmark Genetics) as core analytical infrastructure; not farmer-facing products |
| **SNP genotyping platforms** (e.g., Affymetrix/ThermoFisher salmon SNP chips) | Selective Breeding & Genetics | Generates the raw genomic data (SNP calls) that breeding-value software consumes | ThermoFisher/Affymetrix, Illumina-based platforms (global genomics vendors, not Norwegian) | Lab hardware + vendor bioinformatics software, not a farm-facing platform | No — lab equipment, disconnected from farm IoT entirely | Used by Norwegian breeding companies (AquaGen, Benchmark Genetics/SalmoBreed) as standard genotyping infrastructure |
| **Altinn — Fiskeridirektoratet reporting forms** (e.g., "Månedsrapport biomasse," "Miljørapportering") | Production Planning (regulatory) | Mandatory monthly biomass reporting and environmental monitoring reporting, feeding Norway's official MAB compliance and biomass statistics | Government (Altinn is Norway's national digital public-service portal; forms owned by Fiskeridirektoratet, copies routed to Mattilsynet and the Environment Agency) | Web-based portal, manual form submission | No — manual/periodic form entry, not sensor-connected | National-scale, mandatory for every licensed site; **being phased out** — Altinn submission for biomass reporting is scheduled to end in June 2026, moving fully to machine-to-machine API reporting |
